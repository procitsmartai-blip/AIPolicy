GAR Procurement G-GCC-P-018 Policy Guide

Section 1

What the AI Policy means for Procurement

G-GCC-P-018 — effective 29 April 2026. Applies to all employees and third parties acting on behalf of the company.

What is this policy and what is AI?
Objective: G-GCC-P-018 provides governance on the principles, requirements, use, and development of AI systems within Golden Agri-Resources Ltd and all subsidiaries. It applies to all employees and third parties — including consultants and service providers — acting on behalf of the company for any acquired AI system. Effective 29 April 2026.
Definition of AI

Machine-based systems that infer from input data to generate predictions, recommendations, decisions, or content. These outputs may be used by the company to deliver measurable business benefit.

Productivity & Analytics Driven AI
Enables business productivity gains without structural process changes. Examples: Co-Pilot, departmental Gen-AI, IoT sensor analytics, forecasting tools.
Governed by Group IT
Process Level AI Agents
Automates defined steps in end-to-end business processes using rule- or parameter-driven logic. Operates semi-autonomously under human oversight.
Governed by Group IT
Enterprise Autonomous & Prescriptive AI
Strategic AI embedded in enterprise decision-making and execution. Uses prescriptive analytics and optimisation engines across multiple systems.
Requires executive oversight
SaaS Embedded AI
Built-in AI within off-the-shelf SaaS solutions (e.g. Workday, Coupa). Operates in a contained manner within the SaaS application.
Contained within SaaS

Approved tools only

Only Group IT-approved AI tools may be used for company work. Personal or free subscriptions are banned for business data.

Humans stay accountable

AI output is advisory only. Every AI-assisted evaluation or decision requires a documented human review step.

Check data before you paste

Assess data confidentiality before inputting into any AI tool. Strictly Confidential data requires special approval.

Allowed
Enterprise Microsoft Copilot — drafting, summarising, translating
AI-suggested evaluation criteria with human finalisation
Spend analytics via Group IT-approved tools
Coupa built-in AI (confirm feature scope with Group IT)
Not allowed
ChatGPT (free/personal), Claude.ai personal, Gemini personal
Confidential bid pricing into AI without data classification check
AI-automated supplier ranking or award without human review
Any tool not on the Group IT approved list
Policy effective 29 April 2026 — no grace period. Non-compliance is subject to disciplinary action up to termination (Sec 32). Audit your tools now.

Section 4

Open questions

These must be resolved before procurement can fully operationalise the policy. Raise in the next relevant forum before the next sourcing cycle begins.

A — Group IT
Q1
What is the SLA for Group IT to evaluate and approve or reject a new AI tool request?
Procurement needs to know the waiting period before using a new AI-powered sourcing tool. An undefined timeline creates sourcing delays.
Direct toGroup IT
Q2
Which specific Coupa and Microsoft Copilot features and plugins are covered under the approved list? Is there a written register?
The policy approves these platforms but not all features. Procurement uses both extensively and needs clarity to avoid inadvertent non-compliance.
Direct toGroup IT
Q3
How is a data confidentiality assessment operationally performed before inputting data into an AI tool — self-service checklist or formal sign-off per document type?
Without a defined process, procurement staff will either skip this step or interpret it inconsistently.
Direct toGroup IT
B — Management / Policy owner
Q4
Where is the line between AI as "advisory" and AI "automating" a procurement decision? Can AI-generated supplier scoring be used as a human-adjusted starting point?
The policy prohibits automating business decisions but does not define "automate" in a procurement context.
Direct toPolicy owner / GCC
Q5
Is there a transition plan for existing AI vendor contracts that do not meet Section 26 requirements — renegotiate at next renewal, or proactively before expiry?
The policy is effective now but existing contracts cannot be changed unilaterally. Procurement cannot prioritise remediation without a management position.
Direct toManagement / AI Committee
C — Legal
Q6
What are the acceptable data residency geographies for each of GAR's operating jurisdictions — Indonesia, Singapore, and China?
Section 26(a) requires data residency to be contractually explicit, but the policy does not specify acceptable countries. Without this, Schedule B in the contract addendum cannot be completed.
Direct toLegal
Q7
Does SaaS Embedded AI (e.g. Coupa, Workday) require the same Section 26 contractual protections, or are they exempt given they operate in a "contained manner"?
This has significant renegotiation implications. If exempt, procurement's contract remediation workload reduces substantially. If not, Coupa and Workday agreements need urgent review.
Direct toLegal
D — Group Controllership & Finance
Q8
For High Investment Value initiatives (≥ USD 1M), how does the AI Committee approval process interact with G-BPC-P-004 Investment Committee Governance — sequential, parallel, or does one supersede the other?
Procurement needs the correct sequencing to plan sourcing timelines. If both committees are required and meet on different cadences, this directly affects lead time before contract execution.
Direct toGroup Controllership / GCC
